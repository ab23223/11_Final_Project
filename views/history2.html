What are the issues with Climate Change?

And how can we fix them??

Lets find out

1992 🌍

Earth Summit in Rio de Janeiro raises global awareness about climate change and biodiversity.

1997 🏭

Kyoto Protocol is adopted, committing countries to reduce greenhouse gas emissions.

2005 🔥

Record-breaking heatwaves in Europe; climate scientists warn of intensifying global warming.

2008 🌊

Rising sea levels continue to threaten low-lying islands and coastal cities worldwide.

2010 🌪️

Hurricane season highlights the increased frequency and intensity of storms linked to climate change.

2015 🏖️

Paris Agreement signed: nations agree to limit global warming to below 2°C.

2017 🌡️

Wildfires and heatwaves devastate several continents, emphasizing the urgency for action.

2020 🦠

Global lockdowns due to COVID-19 temporarily reduce emissions, highlighting human impact.

2021 🌱

IPCC releases report: human activity is “unequivocally” the cause of global warming.

2023 🌊

Rising seas and extreme weather continue to threaten ecosystems and human populations.

What are the Issues?

Climate Change is creating many issues for our planet, at this rate if we do not do anything to slow down Climate change, the effects below will continue to get worse.

  • 🌡️ Hotter temperatures.
  • 🌊 More severe storms.
  • 🥀 Increased drought.
  • 💧 A warming, rising ocean.
  • 🐘 Loss of species.
  • 🍕 Not enough food.
  • 😷 More health risks.
  • 🙍 Poverty and displacement.
  • 🪸 Coral bleaching.
  • 🌱 Reduced soil health.

Why It Matters

Climate change matters because it threatens human health, safety, and livelihoods, and negatively impacts all life on Earth.

It leads to more extreme weather events, rising sea levels, and disrupted ecosystems and economies.

It creates escalating challenges for populations, especially in vulnerable regions, making it essential to act now to reduce emissions and build resilience.

Truths & Myths

🌍 Myth: The Earth’s climate has always changed
❗ Fact: Climate change now is happening much faster than natural patterns and is driven mainly by human activities like burning fossil fuels and deforestation.
❄️ Myth: Global warming isn’t real because it’s still cold
❗ Fact: Weather is short-term and can vary; climate is long-term. Global warming can still cause cold extremes due to shifting patterns, even if overall temperatures rise.
🔥 Myth: Heatwaves and wildfires aren’t linked to climate change
❗ Fact: Climate change makes heatwaves and wildfires more frequent and intense by creating hotter, drier conditions that help fires spread.
🇨🇳 Myth: China is mostly responsible for climate change
❗ Fact: Climate change is a global issue. Historically, richer Western nations have caused more emissions. China’s per-person emissions are lower than the USA’s, and it’s investing heavily in renewables.
🌱 Myth: Plants need carbon dioxide, so more is good
❗ Fact: CO₂ is natural, but too much is harmful. Current levels are the highest in 3 million years, and forest loss means there’s less absorption of excess CO₂.
🦉 Myth: Animals will adapt to climate change
❗ Fact: Some can adapt or migrate, but the speed of change and habitat loss mean many species can’t keep up.
💰 Myth: Renewable energy is more expensive
❗ Fact: Solar and wind are now cheaper than oil, gas, or nuclear in most cases — sometimes up to 9× cheaper.
⚡ Myth: Renewables only work when sunny or windy
❗ Fact: New tech stores energy and balances the electricity grid, so renewables can work even when the sun or wind are low.
⏳ Myth: Climate change is a future problem
❗ Fact: Climate change is already causing food shortages, disasters, and migration. Immediate action is needed to meet 1.5°C targets and avoid worse impacts.
🌐 Myth: Climate change is too big to solve
❗ Fact: Solutions exist: renewable energy, ending deforestation, and reducing emissions. Positive action improves health, biodiversity, and energy security.
